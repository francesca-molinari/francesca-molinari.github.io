I am Vice Dean for Education and Professor at the [Johns Hopkins University School of Government and Policy](https://sgp.jhu.edu/). My research interests are in econometrics, both theoretical and applied.

My theoretical work studies identification problems and develops methods for statistical inference in partially identified models. My applied work focuses primarily on decision-making under risk and uncertainty, including the estimation of risk preferences using market-level data and the analysis of individuals’ probabilistic expectations using survey data.

I am an editor of the [*Journal of Political Economy*](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/toc/jpe/current/) and a former joint managing editor of the [*Review of Economic Studies*](https://academic.oup.com/restud). I am a Fellow of both the Econometric Society and the International Association for Applied Econometrics, and a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.

I received my Ph.D. from Northwestern University after obtaining my B.A. and master's in economics, respectively, from the Università degli Studi di Torino and CORIPE Piemonte in Italy.
